Notable Cases

The Tissue Blanket Killer (TBK)

Also known as Acustragulum (Latin fusion for "needle and blanket"), this is Heiki's most notorious case which lasted for two years. The killer harvested individual tissues from victims, keeping them in pink buckets to create human tissue blankets. By the time Heiki confronted him, Acustragulum had already sold over 9,000 blankets (each requiring three 5'5" people). Heiki discovered 94 unsold blankets at the crime scene, with the killer working on a 95th.

This is the only criminal Heiki has ever killed, resulting in severe injuries including damage to his shoulder that left a massive scar.

The Dancer Case

What began as a simple request from a father to help his crying dancer son evolved into one of Heiki's deepest cases, as the dancer was suffering from depression and anxiety.

The Chocolate Kidnapper

Heiki found a child's stolen chocolate in a kidnapper's pocket, leading to the discovery that the criminal was planning to use the treats to lure the child. The kidnapper had stolen all the child's chocolate as part of his plan.

The Missing Cat

Heiki's first ever solved case as a young detective – he successfully located someone's lost pet cat.

The 20-Year Disappearance

An unresolved case that still haunts Heiki – a person who went missing 20 years ago whom he has never been able to locate despite his best efforts.

The Blinded Criminal

In one of his more unusual tactical moments, Heiki threw chocolate at a criminal, which melted and temporarily blinded them, allowing for their capture.

The Bank Robbery

One of Heiki's fastest cases – he caught the robber of a world-class bank in just 7 minutes and 21 seconds.

Character Traits

Dedicated Professional

Works day and night shifts, rarely sleeps, relies on caffeine (90-120 coffees daily) and takes 24 pills per day to maintain his lifestyle.

Unstoppable Determination

Once fought with both legs fractured. Will continue fighting until death. After the protagonist's sacrifice (true ending), he shows no reaction but works both night and day shifts, refusing to throw away the protagonist's school notebooks.

Protective Guardian

Extremely protective of the protagonist, whom he's cared for since they were 2 years old. Flies personally to collect the protagonist after their parents' deaths.

Collaborative Worker

Contrary to the lone wolf stereotype, he's collaborative with other officers and asks for help when needed.

Practical Dresser

Always wears his tie because he can use it to suffocate criminals if necessary. Keeps a pen in his mouth like a cigarette to both write notes and potentially stab someone if needed.

Anti-Substance

Avoids alcohol and cigarettes, finding them "inhumane." His only addiction is caffeine.

Sleep Enthusiast

Ironically, his favorite activity is sleeping. Owns the most customized and comfortable bed known to man, despite rarely getting to use it.

PTSD & Coping Mechanisms

Heiki suffers from severe PTSD after the Tissue Blanket Killer case. His trauma manifests in several ways:

  • • Uses the protagonist as a coping mechanism, focusing on their innocence as an escape from his dark experiences
  • • Avoids restaurants and never buys fried chicken from them, as the smell of dead chicken triggers flashbacks
  • • Perfected his own fried chicken recipe so others won't ask him to order from restaurants
  • • Conceals emotional outbursts, going to the roof to scream for hours when overwhelmed
  • • Plays board games to maintain a sense of control
  • • Keeps a personal diary where he documents his thoughts (which the protagonist can photocopy if they catch him away from home)
  • • Experiences severe anxiety around blankets due to the TBK case, despite his love of sleeping

When his coping mechanisms fail, Heiki simply smiles and gives up temporarily, saving his breakdown for his next rooftop "scream session." If caught during one of these sessions, he freezes completely before deflecting by scolding the protagonist for being awake past bedtime.

How He Stopped The Tissue Blanket Killer

The final confrontation with Acustragulum (TBK) was one of the most harrowing experiences of Heiki's career:

The Raid

Heiki led a squad of six officers into TBK's hideout. They split up with officers checking the basement and upper floors while Heiki remained on the main floor. Soon after separating, screams echoed through the building. Using an echolocation device, Heiki discovered one officer with their head separated from their body, meticulously cleaned of blood. More screams followed as the remaining officers were eliminated one by one.

The Confrontation

Following the sound of a needle at work, Heiki entered a room to find TBK arranging a blanket made of skin while extracting tissues from a pink bucket. The killer's face was embedded with needles. When Heiki ordered him to surrender, TBK merely asked "say please?" before throwing a needle into Heiki's leg, dragging him across the room using yarn, shooting each of his fingers individually, and throwing him across the room.

The Pursuit

Despite severe injuries, Heiki's adrenaline kept him going. He threw a corkboard at TBK, who stitched up his own wounds before fleeing to the roof. Heiki pursued him up the stairs despite his injuries.

The Final Showdown

On the roof, TBK threw a human-tissue blanket at Heiki, triggering traumatic recognition of the smell. As TBK attempted to jump-attack him, Heiki punched him in the face, knocking out several teeth including canines. TBK stabbed these teeth into Heiki's spinal cord, paralyzing parts of his body while saying "you could have said please..."

The Kill

With parts of his body paralyzed, Heiki drew a hidden second gun and shot TBK directly in the heart. As his body gave out, Heiki fell from the five-story building. TBK's final words were "you could have let me finish the 95th one and then killed me."

The Rescue

Xa Yoho, Heiki's assistant, caught him mid-air despite having a fractured arm himself, saving Heiki's life in a dramatic fashion.
